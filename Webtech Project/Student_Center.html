Clubs

School Clubs at CIFS
At CIFS, Our aim is not only to develop intellectual aspects of personality, but to supplement & refine students’ personality with Co-Curricular activities and nourish them to superior qualities such as aesthetic development, character building, spiritual growth, physical growth, moral values, creativity, to develop co-ordination, adjustment, speech fluency, extempore expressions, etc. And to achieve this goal, we have divided the whole strength of students into the following clubs.
Keeping this in mind, we have launched a few Clubs and the details for the same are given below:
1. M.F Hussain’s Club:
Art ignites and takes young minds on a creatively fulfilling journey. Our Art Club exposes children to different creative experiences. The students learn about different kinds of art like Abstract Painting, Lino Art, Collage Making and many more.
2. Music and Dance Club:
Music lessons not only foster a love of music, but also help children in dealing with stress and nervousness.Therefore to make them feel the rhythm of music, we have the Music and Dance Club where they learn different dance forms like Patriotic Dance, Lavani Dance of Maharashtra, Bihu Dance of Assam, Kalbeliya Dance of Haryana and Ghoomar Dance of Gujarat.
3. Literary Club:
Every child is good in one or the other field. The Literary club exposes the students to varied forms associated with literature and it’s several other dynamic branches. We also prepare students for Role Plays, Mime, and Stage Performances etc in all languages.
4. Vishvanathan Club:
Running is not a sport for everyone. It is about the sweat in your hair and the blisters on your feet. It is about throbbing calves and cramps at midnight. At CIFS, we motivate our students to get outdoors and run, when the whole world is only dreaming about it. In our Club, we have Indoor Games like Table Tennis, Chess, Badminton and outdoor games like Basket Ball, Kho- Kho and many more to keep our students physically fit and active and to enhance sportsman spirit in them.

Expeditions/Educational Trips/Adventure Camps

Technology has proved to be a boon for us. Everything is being solved with a click of button. But when we physically experience anything , it remains in our mind and heart forever. Cambridge believes in conceptual knowledge and practicality. So keeping this thing in mind , Cambridge International Foundation School organized an educational trip to Bhakra Nangal Dam under the guidance of Mr. Amandeep Singh and Mr. Ajay Wadhwa. This trip eased students and made them clear about the doubts lingering in their minds. It also helped them in scoring marks in their final exams. It was a lifelong learning for students.
Visit to ICP at Amritsar was another amazing experience where the students could encounter the patriotic feelings in their heart. Easing out of trade regulation between the India and Pakistan was discussed with students.

Partnership with Parents

We understand that parents’ active participation in the activities of their children in school is essential if we are to flourish as community. We would like the parents’ experience to be a positive one. Parents are kept informed of all school events through a newsletter. They follow their child’s progress through regular and detailed school reports. We also welcome parents to visit our schools to discuss matters of concern with House Tutor/House-mother. This way we ensure that everyone contributes to be outstanding development of students. It is a Parents-Teachers interaction that the most important contributing factor to a child’s success at school, sharing responsibility for a creating a working relationship helps children succeed academically. Cambridge International School communicating with parents effectively on Parents’ Teachers Meeting conducted four times in year, orientations and special events organized by Parents Teachers Association of Cambridge such as social gatherings, annual day celebration, workshops, seminars, concerts, national holiday celebrations. On Parents’ Teachers Meeting we communicating with parents regarding school policies and services, informing them about learning process The foundation for good parent-teacher relationships is frequent and open communication, mutual respect and a clear understanding of what is best for each individual child.

Awards & Recognition

Cambridge International AS and A Levels, Cambridge IGCSE and Cambridge O Levels have long been recognised by the Association of Indian Universities (AIU) and this information is available in the ‘Equivalence of Foreign degrees’ published by the AIU. Universities and colleges in India are autonomous but in general follow the recommendations of the AIU.
These are as follows:
• five passes (including English) at grades C or above at GCE O Level or IGCSE and two or three passes at GCE A Level
• students intending to join medical or engineering degree programmes are required to have obtained: A Level passes in Physics, Chemistry, Biology/ Mathematics and English (AS Level) in addition to five passes at GCE O Level/IGCSE.
