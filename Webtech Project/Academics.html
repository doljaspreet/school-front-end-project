Curriculum

All Cambridge International Schools are member affiliates of CBSE (Central Board of Secondary Education, New Delhi) and IGCSE (International General Certificate of Secondary Education, University of Cambridge, UK) for Secondary and Senior Secondary school examination in Grades X and XII.

Keeping up with the commitment to provide global educational experiences Cambridge International Schools offers IGCSE for AS (Grade IX and Grade X) and A level (Grade XI and Grade XII).

Play School and Kindergarten

The first few years of learning are perhaps the most crucial for every child. At Cambridge International Schools, we understand this fact very well. A friendly dedicated staff and a stimulating environment ensure that children feel safe and free to explore. We believe that play is the most important tool of learning and problem-solving for a child during his/her formative years. Hence, our curriculum includes lots of games. Our pre-school classrooms are well equipped with materials that encourage imagination and socialization – including books, blocks, manipulative, paints, dramatic play, eurhythmics, kinaesthetic and sensory activity. This we believe is the foundation every child needs as he/she moves into the academic area of language art, reading, mathematics, science, social studies, etc.

Primary School and Secondary

At the primary and the secondary level, our schools lay extra emphasis on a development-based curriculum. Special attention is given on inculcating optimistic attitude and healthy habits for a positive self-esteem. It is at this stage of education that we encourage children to constructively question things around them. And this is also the stage where we make the teaching-learning process more personalized and meaningful. Because children are at an age where they pick up things very quickly, we also offer them the option of learning an additional language - Indian and foreign - apart from English - the language of instruction.

As children continue to learn and grow, we regularly monitor they development and share it with parents. Hence, we work together with parents since the very beginning. Medium of Instruction The teaching methodology incorporates a student-centered system of learning. While English is the medium of instruction, students have a third language in middle school. In Grades XI and XII, students can choose from a variety of subjects in the fields of Science, Commerce & Humanities.

IB DP Curriculum

The Diploma Programme offered by the International Baccalaureate is a trailblazer in the field of education. It offers a unique experience of academic excellence which is in sharp contrast to the conventional CBSE and ICSE boards. It is a dynamic curriculum, where learning by rote is not encouraged and students discover for themselves the immense joy of learning. Under the supervision and guidance of experienced staff they challenge myths and ideas, thus becoming critical thinkers and analysts with a mind of their own. They are exposed to contemporary issues and modern works of literature. This brings them to the threshold of the world at large- a world without boundaries- and they learn to become responsible global citizens of tomorrow.

Awards & Recognition

Cambridge International AS and A Levels, Cambridge IGCSE and Cambridge O Levels have long been recognised by the Association of Indian Universities (AIU) and this information is available in the ‘Equivalence of Foreign degrees’ published by the AIU. Universities and colleges in India are autonomous but in general follow the recommendations of the AIU.
These are as follows:
• five passes (including English) at grades C or above at GCE O Level or IGCSE and two or three passes at GCE A Level
• students intending to join medical or engineering degree programmes are required to have obtained: A Level passes in Physics, Chemistry, Biology/ Mathematics and English (AS Level) in addition to five passes at GCE O Level/IGCSE.
